Sustainable Aluminum Recycling on Mars

Aluminum structures can be recycled by using olivine sand (abundantly available in Jezero Crater) as foundry sand to cast aluminum objects. By using concentrated solar furnaces capable of melting mission-derived aluminum scrap, we can produce molten metal for casting. Essential objects like sprinklers and tools can be produced, which is highly critical for long-term Mars mission self-sufficiency and resource utilization.
This has a difficulty factor of 2/3.

1. Collection 📦

Process: Manual Retrieval from Cargo & Habitat Supplies.

Action: Gather leftover aluminum structures (e.g., framework, packaging) left after habitat inflation and separate them from other waste materials for processing.

2. Cleaning ✨

Process: Removal of Contaminants.

Action: Remove dust, dirt, or any chemical residues to prepare the aluminum for melting and subsequent casting use, ensuring structural integrity.

3. Shredding 🔪

Process: Mechanical Shredding.

Action: Cut or shred large aluminum pieces into smaller fragments or flakes to maximize surface area, making the metal easier and more energy-efficient to melt in the furnace.

4. Melting 🔥

Process: Melting of aluminum using concentrated solar furnaces.

Action: Place the metal shreds in the solar furnace and concentrate solar energy until the aluminum reaches its melting point, creating pure molten metal.

5. Creating Molds 🏗️

Process: Creating molds using Jezero Crater regolith (olivine sand).

Action: Use olivine sand, abundantly available on Mars, as a foundry material to create precision molds for various products like utensils and essential habitat sprinklers.

6. Creating Final Product 🛠️

Process: Pouring molten aluminum into the molds.

Action: Carefully pour the molten aluminum into the regolith molds. Allow the metal to cool and solidify before separating the finished product from the mold material.
