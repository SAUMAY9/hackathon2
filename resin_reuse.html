Reuse of Resin in Habitat Construction

Collected plastic resin, after sorting, cleaning, and size reduction, can be transformed into usable habitat components rather than being discarded. **Thermoplastics** can be remelted into sheets for bladders and patches or extruded into filaments for 3D printing connectors, valves, and anchors. **Thermoset plastics** or contaminated materials can be ground into powder and incorporated as fillers in composite prints. After deployment, any undamaged sheets, films, or printed parts can be salvaged and reused for repairs, spare components, or new fabrication. This creates a closed-loop recycling system, maximizing material efficiency and reducing waste in the habitat construction process.
The main components of plastic resin are Thermoset/Thermoplastic Resin - 40%, Carbon Fibre - 60%.

1. Collection & Quick Triage 🗂️

Action: Separate likely thermoplastics (LDPE, HDPE, PP, PET, ABS, PLA) from thermosets (epoxy, cured resins).

2. Cleaning 🧼

Action: Remove contaminants using water + biodegradable detergent or wet wipes.

3. Size Reduction 📐

Action: Shred/chop to 5–10 mm flakes (manual shredder, blade grinder, or scissors).

4. Decision: Type of Plastic? 🤔

Thermoplastic Paths:

  • Path A: Melt-Sheeting / Slab Extrusion → Produce sheets/films → bladders & patches
  • Path B: Filament Extrusion → Produce filament → FDM print connectors, valves, anchors

Thermoset or Contaminated Plastics:

  • Grind to powder → Use as filler in printed composites (with virgin binder)
  • Or store for energy/chemical recycling (not for demo)

5. Finishing & Fabrication 🛠️

  • Heat/ultrasound weld seams of sheets
  • Laminate layers for barrier & strength
  • 3D print connectors/valves
  • Assemble & perform leak/pressure checks

6. Reuse Loops 🔄

  • Salvage printed connectors & undamaged film after habitat inflation
  • Use for repair patches or spare parts
