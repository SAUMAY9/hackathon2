Reuse of Resin in Habitat Construction

Collected plastic resin, after sorting, cleaning, and size reduction, can be transformed into usable habitat components rather than being discarded. **Thermoplastics** can be remelted into sheets for bladders and patches or extruded into filaments for 3D printing connectors, valves, and anchors. **Thermoset plastics** or contaminated materials can be ground into powder and incorporated as fillers in composite prints. After deployment, any undamaged sheets, films, or printed parts can be salvaged and reused for repairs, spare components, or new fabrication. This creates a closed-loop recycling system, maximizing material efficiency and reducing waste in the habitat construction process.
The main components of plastic resin are Thermoset/Thermoplastic Resin - 40%, Carbon Fibre - 60%.
